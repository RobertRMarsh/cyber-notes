## Notes
### Social Engineering
- for cyber attackers; it's a low investment gamble with a potentially high payoff
- applied overtime can extract significant insider knowledge about almost any org/individual
#### Tactics
##### Vishing
- rogue IVR(Interactive Voice Response) that re-creates a legitimate bank or other org's IVR system
- could be prompted to call through a *smishing (sms phishing)* or phishing email
- often rejects logins multiple times hoping users try multiple of their passwords
- advanced ones could transfer to a human posing as a customer service agent 
##### Quid pro quo
- requesting password or login credentials in exchange for something
##### Pretexting
- impersonating a authority figure or trusted individual to gain access to a system/information
	- could be claiming to be IT doing maintenance
##### Tailgating
- following an authorized user to a restricted area
	- physically, could be asking someone to hold the door
	- technically, could be borrowing a phone or laptop to do something, but actually installs malicious software on 
##### Shoulder surfing
- directly observing someone enter their credentials
