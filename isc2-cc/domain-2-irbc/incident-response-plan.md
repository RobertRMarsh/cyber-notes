## Notes
- preserves business viability and survival.
- Aimed at reducing impact of an incident to resume normal operations ASAP, speeds up *DRP* by reducing damage that it must recover
- subset of *BCP (Business Continuity Plan)*
- A *Redbook* is a hard copy of IRP incase power is knocked out (kept outside facility)
- All orgs must be prepared for incidents despite best efforts to avoid them
- Priorities; protect life, health, safety, and always choose safety first
### Components of an IRP 
#### Preparation
- developing a policy approved by management 
- identifying critical data/systems/single points of failure
- training staff for incident response
- implementing an incident response team
- practicing *Incident Identification* (first response)
- identifying roles/responsibilities 
- planning the coordination of communication between stakeholders
- considering the possibility that a primary method of communication may not be available
#### Detection/analysis
- monitoring all possible attack vectors
- analyzing the incident using known data and threat intelligence 
- prioritizing incident response 
- standardizing incident documentation
#### Containment eradication and recovery
- gathering evidence 
- choose an appropriate containment strategy
- identify the attacker
- isolate the attack
#### Post incident recovery
- identify evidence that may need to be retained 
- document lessons learned
- conduct a retrospective of this and all previous steps
