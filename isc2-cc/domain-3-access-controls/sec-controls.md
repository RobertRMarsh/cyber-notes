## Notes
### What is a security control?
- control is a safeguard/countermeasure that preserves CIA of data/assets
- *access controls* limit objects availability to subjects according to rules/parameters
	- *access controls* not only restrict access but also permit access
### Controls and Risks
- controls reduce risk to an org 
	- administrative controls are laws 
		- i.e. law is wearing a seatbelt
	- physical control 
		- i.e. the seatbelt
	- technical control
		- i.e. car beeping at you if you don't wear the seatbelt
### Monitoring 
#### Cameras 
- flexible monitoring
- should be combined with other sensors can provide evidence if recording
#### Logs
- physical logs; sign in sheets
- logs should not be kept indefinitely, likely six months or something
- *log anomaly* is anything out of the ordinary, 
	- like in login and logout dates
#### Alarms Systems
- trigger when something unauthorized occurs
- *panic buttons* call the police, and security personnel
#### Security Guards
- effective physical security control that is discouraging for a threat actor
