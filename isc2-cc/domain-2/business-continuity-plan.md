## Notes
- Meant to maintain business operations during incident/disaster
- not specifically focused on IT or returning to NOP(Normal Operating Procedure)
- executive management is responsible for providing support for *business continuity planning*
### Components of a BCP
- Multiple contact methods for BCP members/backup members
- Immediate Response Procedures/checklists:
	- fire suppression procedures
	- security and safety procedures
	- notification of appropriate emergency-response agencies, etc.
	- Notification systems and call trees for alerting personnel BCP is underway
		- *Call trees*, when one person is unavailable the caller knows who else to contact
	- Guidance for management, including designation of authorities for specific managers
		- how/when to enact plans
	- Contact numbers for critical supply chain members
#### BIA (Business Impact Analysis)
- Identifying functions and dependencies in org.
- helps to flush out BCP
### BC Emergency
- for communication outages from cyber attacks related to critical infrastructure like hospitals military grade networks can be used to maintain essential activity
